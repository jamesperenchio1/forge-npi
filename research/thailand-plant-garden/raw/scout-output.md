## ANGLE 1: Common Crops, Vegetables, Herbs, and Ornamental Plants

**Most-grown categories:**
- Herbs (ubiquitous): Holy basil (*mamuang*), Thai sweet basil, lemongrass, galangal, ginger, cilantro, garlic chives, dill
- Vegetables: Morning glory (*pak boong*), bok choy, yard-long beans, cucumber, chilli (bird's eye + finger-length), cabbage, pak choi, broccoli, celery
- Native/wild greens (*pak phuen baan*): Ivy gourd (*tamlung*), moringa (*dok kajon/marum*), soap pod
- Fruits/vines: Banana, pumpkin, squash
- Ornamentals/collector plants: Orchids, aroids (Monstera, Philodendron, Anthurium, Alocasia, Hoya), plumeria/frangipani, bougainvillea

**Location-specific:**
- **Bangkok**: Balcony/rooftop herb gardens common. High-rises introduce intense wind + heat. One documented 5th-floor balcony garden had 1,000+ plants starting with morning glory and basil. Clay-heavy soils in central Bangkok flood plains.
- **Chiang Mai**: Cooler winters (8–15°C at night Nov–Feb), better for cool-season crops (lettuce, cabbage, strawberries). **But**: Feb–April smoke/haze season (PM2.5 10–15x WHO limits). Air quality compromises outdoor plant health + gardening activity.
- **Coastal (Phuket, Hua Hin, Pattaya)**: Salt spray, sandy soils, strong sea winds. Salt-tolerant choices: casuarina, Indian almond (*Terminalia catappa*), barringtonia, bougainvillea, frangipani. Salt-sensitive edibles struggle.

**Collector plant culture**: Thailand is a *global export hub* for rare aroids. The Bangkok International Exotic Plants Show (BIEPS) draws international buyers. Thai Exotic Greenery, BK Interplants, Greenroom Planter — all export rare variegated Monstera, Philodendron, Anthurium worldwide. Monstera Thai Constellation was *created in a Thai lab*. Serious collectors in Bangkok are highly active and globally networked.

**Sources**: CityFarmer, Gardening Know How, Thailand NOW wild greens, Bangkok City Farm, BIEPS Guide, Thai Exotic Greenery, BK Interplants

## ANGLE 2: Climate Challenges (UV, Monsoon, Heat, Wind, Humidity)

**Heat:**
- Bangkok avg 28°C year-round; max 35–40°C+ from March–May
- Heat accelerates organic matter decomposition in soil — organic manure loses nutrients faster than in temperate climates, requiring more frequent lower-dose applications
- Soil in pots loses moisture *rapidly* in Bangkok heat; dark plastic pots become plant-killers

**UV:**
- Intense UV year-round at latitude ~13°N. Solar noon ~12:00–12:30 PM local time. Peak UV: 10 AM–2 PM
- Causes leaf scorch, damages seedlings, accelerates evaporation
- 40–50% shade netting common fix; HDPE shade nets widely sold in Thailand market

**Monsoon:**
- Southwest Monsoon: May–October. Bangkok floods periodically in Sept–Oct
- Heavy rains waterlog beds, drown roots, create widespread fungal conditions
- Monsoon timing is increasingly erratic: 2022 saw severe drought followed by flash floods within weeks

**Chiang Mai-specific:**
- Burning season Feb–April: PM2.5 averages above 100 μg/m³ in March; peak AQI hit 223 in 2023 (15x WHO limit)
- Reduces photosynthesis, harms foliage, limits outdoor gardening activity

**Rooftop/balcony wind:**
- High-rise buildings create wind tunnels; plants at height face sustained wind stress beyond ground-level conditions
- Hot, dry winds in dry season cause sudden wilting of balcony plants

**Long-term trend:**
- Thailand temperatures rose 1.04°C average over 40 years (1970–2009)
- Southern Thailand: 60%+ of vegetation degraded in stress years (2005, 2016)

**Sources**: Thaiger gardening guide, Scientific Reports, UNDP Thailand, IQAir Chiang Mai, Al Jazeera Chiang Mai

## ANGLE 3: Real Pain Points from Thai Gardeners

**From Bangkok Post / Mealybug reports:**
- Mealybugs killed 50-year-old rain trees in Bangkok. In 2009 spread to 700+ sq mi of eastern/NE Thailand, killing up to half the plants in affected fields
- Sooty mold follows mealybugs — coats leaves, blocks photosynthesis

**From Tropical Hydroponics Thailand blog (first-person):**
- "Could not find quality net cups locally" — had to improvise with plastic cups
- Expanded polystyrene rafts went waterlogged under crop weight
- "The burning noonday sun would quickly turn an unvented plastic covered High Tunnel into an oven during the dry season"
- Had to engineer shade cloth + ventilation solutions from scratch

**From Thai Garden Design blog:**
- Plants need well-drained AND moisture-retaining soil simultaneously — hard to achieve
- Nitrogen overapplication stunts tropical plants (common mistake from temperate-climate advice)
- Organic manure degrades too fast in heat

**From CityFarmer/Bangkok City Farm:**
- "The urban environment is not really appropriate for growing vegetables"
- Many people give up due to many difficulties + lack of proper methods
- Physical constraints of urban environment defeat most beginners

**Community presence:**
- Facebook Group: Chiang Mai Gardening Forum (active)
- Teak Door Forum: Thailand-specific farming/gardening forum
- No dedicated Thai-language gardening app or community platform found

## ANGLE 5: Hydroponics in Thailand

**Popularity:** Growing but technologically underdeveloped.

- Hydroponic lettuce is mainstream — sold at any Thai retailer
- Home DIY growers in thousands; community on Facebook + Line
- SNAP (Philippines) and Kratky method popular for beginners

**Systems used:**
- **NFT**: Most common commercial; PVC channels, pump-circulated
- **DFT**: Mid-scale commercial
- **Kratky/NCWC**: Most popular for home; no electricity; works in humid tropics
- **DWC**: Larger plants

**Problems:**
- 40% of customers build from blue plastic pipework + cheap foam
- Quality net cups not locally available
- Commercial systems cost over 1M THB/rai; payback >2 years
- Heat management: unventilated greenhouses become ovens in dry season
- "Technical level of Thai horticulture is very low and not well developed"
- Crops: Lettuce dominates; pak choy, kale, morning glory also grown

**Sources**: HortiDaily Thailand, Tropical Hydroponics blog, Frontiers Sustainability 2024

## ANGLE 7: What Makes Serious Plant People Different

**Observed behaviors (Bangkok collector community):**
- Active at BIEPS — buy/sell/trade rare specimens internationally
- Track individual specimens: cutting histories, variegation progression, rooting logs
- Know exact fertilizer NPK ratios for specific species
- Manage humidity actively (humidifiers, misting) for aroids requiring 60%+ RH
- Source chunky aroid mixes (orchid bark + perlite + coco coir)
- Use moss poles/trellis for climbing aroids
- Photograph every new leaf unfurl (each leaf unique in variegated plants)
- Participate in global collector communities (FB groups 10,000s of members)
- Concerned with: individual plant ID, tissue culture vs wild, variegation type authenticity

**Tools they crave:**
- Per-plant photo timeline growth log (not per-species generic care)
- Fertilizer NPK tracking and scheduling
- Humidity + light monitoring integration
- Rare plant collection inventory management
- Thai collector community
- Pest ID with tropical/aroid-specific disease library

## ANGLE 8: Rooftop Gardens — Specific Challenges

**Structural:**
- Bangkok rooftop soil kept at 15cm thickness to manage load
- Trees only over primary columns/beams (structural engineer required)
- Most condos not designed for rooftop gardens; weight is hard constraint

**Environmental:**
- Urban heat island adds several degrees — rooftops 35–40°C+ in dry season
- High-rise wind more severe; hardier, wind-resistant plants required
- Intense sun + wind = rapid soil moisture loss

**Water:**
- Bangkok monsoon — thoughtful design can harvest rainfall
- Most Bangkok condos have no irrigation infrastructure on rooftops

**Inspirational example:**
- Thammasat University Rooftop Farm (TURF): 40+ edible species; each section designed for its specific microclimate (sun exposure, curvature, water flow, elevation, wing orientation)

## ANGLE 10: Thai-Specific Pests, Diseases, and Seasonal Timing

**Mealybugs (biggest documented Thai-specific threat):**
- Most active: Dry season (Dec–Feb). Secondary peak: Oct–Nov (end of rainy season)
- Spreads rapidly "without natural enemies"; killed 50-yr-old trees + devastated 700+ sq mi in 2009
- Sooty mold secondary killer

**Fungus gnats:** Rainy season peak (May–Oct). Target root hairs in moist containers.

**Spider mites:** Hot dry conditions (Nov–Apr). Heat-stressed plants most vulnerable.

**Thrips:** Pierce-and-suck; spread plant viruses. Silvery scars diagnostic.

**Aphids:** Facilitated by ants; common on chillies, herbs.

**Seasonal timing:**

| Month | Key Risk | Climate Event |
|---|---|---|
| Nov–Feb | Mealybug peak | Cool/dry |
| Feb–Apr | Chiang Mai smoke season | PM2.5 hazardous |
| Mar–May | Max heat 40°C+ | Pre-monsoon; spider mite |
| May–Oct | Fungus gnats, root rot, fungal | Monsoon, flooding risk |
| Oct–Nov | Mealybug secondary peak | Late rainy season |

## ADDITIONAL FINDINGS

**Soil:**
- Bangkok: clay-heavy, poor drainage, compacts when dry; requires perlite/sand amendments
- Tropical heat degrades organic content faster — higher amendment frequency

**Fertilizer:**
- Standard temperate nitrogen-heavy advice causes problems in Thailand
- Phosphorus + potash recommended for tropical ornamentals

**Container recommendations:**
- Dark plastic pots absorb heat, cook roots — geo fabric grow bags better
- Shade cloth 40% reduces temp by 10–15°F

**Community:**
- Hydroponics community on Facebook + Line, not purpose-built tools
