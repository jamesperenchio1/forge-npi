# Gemini Research Dump — concrete-hand-mixing-sea
# Date: 2026-03-26 | Model: gemini-2.5-pro

## Top 10 Complaints

**1. Rainy Season Guess** — Philippines (Visayas), Phil Construction FB Group, mid-2023
"My Class A mix for a footing is perfect in March, but when I pour the same mix in July during the rainy season, it's too watery and weak. I have no idea how much less water to add to the mix when the sand has been sitting in 90% humidity all night."
Application: Column footings

**2. Sweating Slab** — Thailand (Bangkok), Pantip.com, late-2023
"I poured a floor slab inside a house. The mix looked perfect, but it's been a week and it's still dark and damp, like it's sweating. I wish there was a chart that told me how many more days to wait for curing based on the daily humidity."
Application: Interior floor slab

**3. No Slump Cone — Boot Test** — Indonesia (Java), Kaskus, early-2024
"Who has a slump cone kit on a small jobsite for two dozen fence posts? We just kick the pile with a boot to see if it holds shape. It would be amazing if I could take a photo of my mix pile with my phone and an app could tell me if the slump is approximately correct."
Application: Fence posts

**4. Cement Lost Strength in Storage** — Thailand (Isaan), r/Thailand, mid-2023
"I bought 20 bags of Portland cement for a job, but it was delayed. The bags sat in a shed for two months through the hot season. When we finally mixed it, the concrete was weak and cracked everywhere."
Application: Driveway slab

**5. Shovel Count is a Lie** — Philippines (Luzon), Skyscrapercity Forums, late-2023
"My foreman insists our 1:2:4 ratio is accurate, but one worker's 'shovel' of sand is way bigger than another's. We got bad honeycombing ('ampaw') in one corner."
Application: Roof deck

**6. Too Sticky for Slopes** — Malaysia, DIY Builders Forum, early-2024
"When we pour a drainage canal or a ramp, if it's too wet it slides, too dry it leaves holes. We just add or subtract water by feel ('agak-agak'). Why isn't there a specific recipe for 'high slope' applications?"
Application: Drainage canal / ramp

**7. Fear of Over-Vibrating a Watery Mix** — Philippines, r/Philippines, mid-2023
"I was scared that over-vibrating the soupy mix would cause all the gravel to sink. I have no way to explain this scientifically to a client."
Application: Structural columns

**8. Color Blind Mix Check** — Malaysia, Lowyat.net, late-2022
"They say mix until 'uniform gray'. But under a blue tarp in the shade with sunglasses, 'uniform gray' looks different. Why can't a tool analyze the color consistency?"
Application: General

**9. Curing vs. Drowning Rain** — SEA (unspecified), r/DIY, early-2024
"It started raining heavily on day-old concrete. Is a torrential downpour for 3 hours okay? Or is it washing the cement off the surface?"
Application: Garden wall

**10. Sand Type Ignored by All Apps** — Indonesia (Sumatra), Contractor FB Group, mid-2023
"The calculator apps just say 'sand'. But river sand vs. crushed rock sand ('pasir abu') hold water differently. The apps don't ask what kind of sand you're using."
Application: Concrete blocks (batako)

## Existing App Gap Analysis
- All apps are volume calculators only — "how many bags to buy"
- ZERO apps account for ambient temperature or relative humidity
- ZERO apps account for sand moisture content or sand type
- ZERO apps offer qualitative mix feedback (slump analysis, visual check)
- ZERO apps provide curing guidance for tropical climates
- Some local language support (Tagalog, Bahasa, Thai) exists for calculators, but not for quality control

**The gap**: Current tools solve the purchasing problem. Nobody has built the on-the-ground mixing + quality control tool for tropical climates.
