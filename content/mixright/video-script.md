# MixRight — Video #1

---

**HOOK** (0-2s):
"bro my concrete failed because it was too humid outside"

[Text on screen, huge, over a shot of cracked concrete floor. No context. Cut immediately.]

---

**SETUP** (2-8s):
[Voiceover, fully unhinged, while camera slowly pans over a half-mixed pile of concrete on a tarp in the sun]

"okay so you know how everyone's out here mixing concrete by vibes? Like they just GUESS how much water to add? In 95% humidity? In Thailand? In July?"

[cut to hands holding a cracked fence post, then cut to hands squeezing a handful of concrete mix — the 'ball test']

"this is a real engineering problem that has affected 40 million people and nobody made an app for it. Until now."

---

**REVEAL** (8-20s):
[Phone screen recording — MixRight app loading. Deep orange header: "MixRight"]
[Voiceover, fast, almost reading a spec sheet]

"okay so. It reads your GPS. Pulls the humidity. It's 88% right now — it's raining season — and it just told me to add ONE LESS LITER of water per bag than normal."

[Screen shows the big orange number: 20.2L per bag. Badge says "GPS"]

"it does the bucket math. Sand. Gravel. How many bags you're mixing."

[Shows bucket count grid: 1 bag / 3.3× sand / 6.7× gravel]

"it even tells you how long to keep it wet after you pour based on today's humidity."

[Curing card: "Keep wet for 10 days. In high humidity, test with a plastic sheet..."]

---

**PAYOFF** (20-35s):
[Voiceover over hands-only shot — hands opening a bag of cement, then typing on phone, then holding up the phone triumphantly in the direction of the concrete pile]

"this is the app that all the YouTube engineers are describing when they say 'adjust for ambient conditions' but then never tell you HOW."

[slow zoom on phone screen]

"it's free. It has no login. It works in Thai. It works in Tagalog. It works offline."

[brief cut to the concrete pile which looks, honestly, pretty normal]

"my columns are not going to have honeycombing anymore and I think that's beautiful."

---

**CTA** (35-40s):
[Text on screen: "mixright.vercel.app"]
[One second of the cracked concrete floor again for contrast]
[End.]

---

**SETTING**: Outside a half-built concrete block house, Isaan region. Red dirt road visible in background. Corrugated metal roof on an adjacent shed. Standard Southeast Asian small construction site energy.

**FILMING STYLE**: 100% hands + phone screen recording. No face. Voiceover recorded separately. Text-on-screen for all spoken words (for muted scroll).

**CAPTION**:
> the concrete calculator that knows it's raining in thailand
> mixright.vercel.app (free, no signup, works in thai/tagalog/bahasa)
> #construction #concreto #DIY #buildingahouse #philippines #thailand #indonesia

**SOUNDS**: Use trending lo-fi phone type beat OR "original audio" — deadpan voiceover with no music is funnier and forces people to unmute.
